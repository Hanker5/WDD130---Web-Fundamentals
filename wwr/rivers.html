The Perfect Rivers to Plunder

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Deep in the Frank Church Wilderness, the Salmon River—dubbed the "River of No Return"—captivates visitors with its rugged beauty, often tempting them to stay. Elk, deer, bears, and bighorn sheep roam the hills, while towering ponderosa pines shade sandy campsites. The river’s rapids, ranging from Class I-IV, shift with the seasons. Early summer brings high, fast, cold waters, while late summer offers slower, warmer flows, perfect for families and swimmers. The Salmon remains one of the longest undammed rivers in the U.S., offering adventure all season long.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

The Green River winds through Utah's desolate canyons, offering stunning desert scenery, towering cliffs, and thrilling Class II/III rapids over an 84-mile stretch from Sand Wash to Swasey’s Rapid. This 6-day trip showcases unique geology, rich history, and evidence of prehistoric life, earning Desolation Canyon its status as a National Landmark.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

The Grand Canyon is the ultimate river adventure, combining thrilling rapids with breathtaking scenery. This iconic trip offers more than just whitewater—it’s a chance to explore hidden side canyons, visit ancient ruins accessible only by river, and immerse yourself in the canyon’s incredible beauty. The rapids are rated on a unique I-X scale, reflecting the variety and intensity of challenges found here. Whether for the adventure or the awe-inspiring landscapes, a Grand Canyon rafting trip is an experience you’ll never forget.
